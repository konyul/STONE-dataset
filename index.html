Abstract

Reliable off-road navigation requires accurate estimation of traversable regions and robust perception under diverse terrain and sensing conditions. STONE is a large-scale multi-modal dataset for off-road robot navigation that provides trajectory-guided 3D traversability maps generated by a fully automated annotation-free pipeline. The dataset includes synchronized 128-channel LiDAR, six RGB cameras, and three 4D imaging radars in a surround-view configuration. It covers diverse environments and conditions, including day and night, grasslands, farmlands, construction sites, and lakes, and establishes a benchmark for voxel-level 3D traversability prediction.

Automatic 3D Traversability Map Generation

1. Dense Surface Reconstruction
Consecutive LiDAR scans are accumulated in the global coordinate system and reconstructed into a dense 3D mesh.
2. Traversability-Oriented Feature Extraction
Geometric features such as elevation, slope, and roughness are extracted from the reconstructed terrain surface.
3. Trajectory-Guided Auto-Labeling
Voxels along the robot trajectory are used as references, and traversability labels are propagated to surrounding regions to generate scalable 3D ground truth.

Description

STONE is designed to support research on robust 3D traversability prediction for off-road robot navigation. Unlike prior datasets that rely on front-view sensing or manual annotation, STONE focuses on scalable annotation-free 3D label generation and comprehensive surround-view multi-modal perception.

The dataset also provides a benchmark for voxel-level 3D traversability prediction under camera-only, LiDAR-only, and multi-modal settings.
